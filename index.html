My Hobby

Korean dramas, or K-dramas, are a global phenomenon known for their emotional storytelling, which often blends romantic elements with themes of friendship, family, and personal growth. These shows span diverse genres like historical (sageuk), fantasy, romance, and thriller, often exploring cultural values and highlighting social issues such as mental health stigma, gender inequality, and corruption. They are celebrated for their compelling narratives and memorable, often inspiring, quotes that encourage viewers to reflect on life, resilience, and the importance of human connection.

History of Kdramas

Nende ajalugu aegade jooksul

Television dramas first appeared in the 1950s, but the genre was limited by early technology.

It began to gain traction in the 1960s as broadcasting companies expanded, laying the foundation for modern K-dramas.

The 1970s saw a rise in production and viewership.

Color television was introduced in 1981, improving visual quality and production values.

Start of the "Korean Wave" or Hallyu. Youth-oriented, soap-opera-style dramas became popular globally.

The internet and streaming platforms distributed K-dramas worldwide, increasing popularity and global engagement.

Current K-dramas often feature faster-paced storytelling and diverse themes. High production values and webtoon adaptations are common.
